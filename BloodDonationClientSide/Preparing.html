Giving blood saves lives. The blood you give is a lifeline in an emergency and for people who need long-term treatments. Many people would not be alive today if donors had not generously given their blood. Around half our current donors are over 45. That's why we need more young people (over the age of 17) to start giving blood, so we can make sure we have enough blood in the future. We need over 6,000 blood donations every day to treat patients

How Can I Prepare?

Before you give blood

  • Check you are able to give blood
  • find appointment
  • drink alot of water and juice
  • eat well to be able to donate without problems

During Donation

  • We will insert a needle in your arm which will collect your blood into a blood bag with your unique number.
  • Ascale weighs the blood and stops when you have donate 470ml.this usually takes between 5-10 minutes.
  • the needle will be remove and sterile dressing applied to your arm.

After you give blood

  • keep the pressure bandage on your arm for about 30 minutes and plaster on for 6 hours.
  • Rest for short time after giving blood .
  • eat and drink-you will be.
  • avoid have ahot bath after you have given blood.
